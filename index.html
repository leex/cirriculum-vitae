Drupal

I started developing with Drupal in late 2010 with my first project being Drupal 6. This was also my last D6 project before moving into D7 nearly soon as it was released. In hindsight it was maybe not right to jump into D7 straight away but it was manageable. I've now completed development of several D7 sites and am very confident with the platform, though there are definitely always things to learn.

I'm a big fan of Drupal for many reasons. Primarily I'm an advocate of open source software. I believe the open source model is a great way to develop as it encourages community and sharing. You don't have the extra layer of complexity where you worry about locking things down so ideas can't be stolen, everything flows freely. This topic moves me into the contrib aspects of Drupal which I think are great, sharing modules, themes and distributions makes Drupal very powerful in it's scope, what it can provide for the developer and ultimately the client.
